The game called "Osu!" is a rythem game with thousands of players every day.
The premise of the game is simple, click the circles to the beat, but in
practice, the game is harder than you would think at first.


Shigetora (aka cookiezi) playing Xi - Freedom Dive.

This website was made to show you the top players of the game.
The players are devided in multiple different classes, jumping, streaming and consistancy.

Jumping is the most obvious one, the better a player can move their cursor across the screen
with good accuracy and speed, the higher a player scores on the leaderboard.
Streaming is when you have to click a lot of circles in rapid succession.
If you look at the video above, you will see a player play a map called: "freedom dive"
The map is a notourious streaming map, with a lot of really hard and fast streams.
So it comes down to this, the faster a player can stream and with good accuracy, the
higher they score.

Consistancy is the hardest one to explain, cause it's based on opinion.
My take on consistancy is the following; when a player plays maps, they get good combos
and get good accuracy. (a combo is when you hit circles in a row, giving more and more
points with the higher combo you get) When a player can get high combos and good accuracy
most of the time, the player is consistant. The more they can get good scores in a row, the
higher the player scores on the leaderboard.
